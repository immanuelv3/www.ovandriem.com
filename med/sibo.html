I have been diagnosed with a suspicion of SIBO/IMO (Intestinal Methanogen Overgrowth) in the winter of 2021. (Addendum, May 24th 22: Additionally to a Gastritis End of May 2022!) {This diagnosis has changed my life, because I was (finally) told what I had (the diagnosis was not made before, although I was tested for it...) (For the insiders: one (of several) testing-factor is that you need to test for methane also in the exhalation-test, hydrogen did not show in a test 9 years ago in my case and generally {for many others!}!) and could start to adjust my diet accordingly, which gave me great relief from many years of suffering and pain. I eventually healed with natural antibiotics, so that I was officially cured from SIBO with a negative test 1 year later.. Nobody had told me that I had (-curable-) problems with my stomach/bowel and made a diagnosis of "IBS" (=Irritable bowel syndrome) with the result that I believed this "condition" could not be cured and not looking for further diagnoses/help.

SIBO is - in my case- a severe disorder of the small intestine and perhaps also stomach, which made me suffer tremendously for about 9 years and also much longer since my teenage-time. Common symptoms are bloating, flatulence, cramps, also skin problems (it made me Akne and very dry skin, that made me itch often).

It´s possible that its cause is an imbalanced and uniform diet, but there are many possible causes that may disturb the intestinal tract... I believe it was the occasional excessive alcohol consumation in my teen-years which lead to my sibo.

When I was diagnosed with SIBO a FODMAP-low diet (FODMAP= Acronym for "fermentable oligo-, di-, monosaccharides and polyols") was recommended to me.

Only months later I found out in another country (France and here the South) that a diet "low in acids and high in basal foods" (which means: no fruit juices, milk products e.i.) would be helpful.

And indeed this diet in combination with FODMAP-low relieved me of the enzymes for the monosaccharides (in fruits), disaccharides (in dairy products) and oligosaccharides (e.g. in bread, beans and more), that I could not live without anymore (and gave me great help because I was able to eat everything again with those, whereas I was restricted to FODMAP-low foods before, that sometimes I could still not tolerate {as is typical and rather normal for the FODMAPs} and made life very difficult in terms of nutrition). I attach a list of beneficial/good and non beneficial/good foods and products under this text for the interested reader.
